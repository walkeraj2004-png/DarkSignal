Why Ecuador's Flower Market?

This market is one of the most geopolitically exposed commodity systems in the world. It depends on U.S. trade policy, phytosanitary regulations (rules governing what plants and plant products can cross borders), Ecuador's hydroelectric power reliability, and tightly timed international logistics.

If the methodology can predict disruption here, where signal complexity is high and operating margins are narrow, it can generalize to other exposed sectors and trade corridors.

World's 3rd largest flower exporter

Upstream policy influence often appears long before formal rule publication.
100,000+ workers
Household livelihoods and regional employment depend on this export ecosystem.

Lobbying Activity Predicts Regulatory Outcomes — 6 Months Early

A Granger causality test asks a simple question: does knowing the history of Variable A help predict the future of Variable B, beyond what B's own history tells you? If yes, A is said to "Granger-cause" B, meaning it contains real predictive information.

In the EFM prototype, lobbying expenditure on agricultural and phytosanitary issues Granger-causes USDA APHIS regulatory output at lag 2 quarters — lobbying activity today predicts regulatory movement roughly 6 months ahead.

The Regulatory Environment Is Tightening

Across 192 APHIS documents, 49.5% score as tightening, 42.4% neutral, and 8.2% loosening. For Ecuador flower exporters, this implies a directional trend toward stricter compliance conditions rather than deregulation.

This Is a Proof of Concept. The Proof Works.

Prototype-scale results have confirmed the core hypothesis: upstream signals in public data contain predictive information about downstream regulatory and market outcomes. The remaining question is not whether the approach works; it is how far it scales under full data volume and full compute.

What MareNostrum 5 Changes

Think of the Stockfish analogy: it does not "predict the future" of a chess game. It evaluates tens of millions of positions per second and identifies moves that remain robust across the widest set of futures. Dark Signal uses supercomputer-scale processing similarly: evaluate millions of geopolitical scenarios simultaneously and map where operational exposure concentrates.

From 10,000 to 1,000,000 scenarios

Monte Carlo simulation scales by 100x, producing calibrated probability distributions instead of directional estimates.

From mock data to live signals

Full CENACE, APHIS, FAS, and AIS pipelines replace prototype fallbacks, with Ecuador field research adding ground-level validation.

From single market to global methodology

The Ecuador pilot validates a framework transferable to any geopolitically exposed commodity market or trade corridor.
